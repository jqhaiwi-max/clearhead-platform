Given your background in e-commerce, digital systems, and your interest in building platforms, I would recommend creating a digital mental health screening platform that follows the same workflow used in many hospitals and mental health clinics. The platform should screen, not diagnose. A licensed clinician should always review the results before making any diagnosis.

Mental Health Screening Workflow

Stage 1 – Patient Registration

Collect:

* Name
* Age
* Gender
* Nationality
* Education
* Occupation
* Marital status
* Emergency contact
* Medical history
* Current medications
* Previous psychiatric diagnosis
* Family psychiatric history

⸻

Stage 2 – General Mental Health Screening

Test	Purpose	Time
Kessler Psychological Distress Scale	Overall mental distress	3 min
World Health Organization Well-Being Index	Well-being	2 min

If distress is low, the assessment can stop or proceed based on clinical judgment.

⸻

Stage 3 – Core Mental Health Screening

Administer:

* Patient Health Questionnaire-9
* Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-item Scale
* Mood Disorder Questionnaire
* Adult ADHD Self-Report Scale
* Obsessive-Compulsive Inventory-Revised
* PTSD Checklist for DSM-5

The system automatically scores each questionnaire.

⸻

Stage 4 – Additional Modules (if indicated)

Only present these if the earlier results suggest they may be relevant.

* Alcohol: Alcohol Use Disorders Identification Test
* Drug use: Drug Abuse Screening Test
* Sleep: Pittsburgh Sleep Quality Index
* Eating disorders: SCOFF Questionnaire

⸻

Stage 5 – Cognitive Screening (optional)

For concerns about memory or cognition:

* Montreal Cognitive Assessment
* Mini-Mental State Examination

⸻

Stage 6 – Risk Assessment

Include clinician-reviewed questions about:

* Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
* Thoughts of harming others
* Psychotic symptoms
* Recent major life events
* Substance use
* Immediate safety concerns

If responses indicate elevated risk, the system should flag the case for urgent clinical review rather than attempting to diagnose.

AI-Assisted Features

A well-designed platform could also:

* Score questionnaires automatically.
* Generate visual graphs showing symptom severity over time.
* Compare current scores with previous visits.
* Produce a structured clinician summary.
* Support multiple languages (e.g., Arabic and English).
* Integrate with appointment scheduling and electronic health records.
* Send follow-up screening reminders to monitor progress.

Important considerations

* Many of these instruments are copyrighted or licensed. You may need permission or a license to reproduce their exact questions in a commercial product.
* The platform should clearly state that it provides screening results, not a medical diagnosis.
* Any AI-generated summaries should be reviewed by a qualified mental health professional before being used in clinical decision-making.

This architecture is similar to the intake process used in many modern mental health clinics and telepsychiatry services, while keeping the clinician responsible for diagnosis and treatment decisions.
